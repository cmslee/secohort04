Egg Drop Soup

This recipe is adapted from Chef Chung Sun Lau's found on Made With Lau. The site is an amazing resource not only for trying out home recipes by a master chef, but also understanding the history of Chinese food in the US. This recipe serves 3.

Ingredients

For soup:

  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp Roasted Chicken Base Better than Bouillon
  • handful of peas (frozen/canned works)
  • handful of corn (frozen/canned works)
  • handful of chopped carrot (a 2-3 inch segment)
  • 1 stalk of green onion

For cornstarch slurry:

  • 4 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch

For egg mixture:

  • 2 eggs
  • 3/4 tbsp water

For flavor:

  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • white pepper (to taste)
  • sesame oil (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Prep veggies. Thaw and/or rinse peas and corn. Cube carrots (to about same size as peas and corn for even cooking). Finely chop green onions.
  2. Set 4 cups of water on stove. Cover and bring to boil. Dissolve Roasted Chicken Better than Bouillon base into boiling water.
  3. Add carrots, peas, and corn. Cover and bring to gentle boil again on medium-high. Partially cover pot and reduce to simmer.
  4. Prep Egg mixture by beating eggs with 3/4 tbsp water for about 20 seconds.
  5. Prep constarch slurry by gently mixing cornstach with 2 tbsp water until smooth.
  6. Slowly pour constarch slurry into simmering soup, stirring constantly.
  7. Slowly pour egg mixture into simmering, thickening soup. Stir constantly and control pouring so that egg "blossoms" into soup.
  8. Turn off heat. Flavor with salt. Add a sprinkle of white pepper and a touch of sesame oil.
  9. Plate the soup in a large serving bowl. Top with chopped green onion and serve immediately.
